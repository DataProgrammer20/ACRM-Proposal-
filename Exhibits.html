History of Computing

Exhibit 1 Photograph*

This exhibit exemplifies the development of computing throughout history. An original Babylonian cuneiform brick and an original Sumerian cuneiform clay tablet can be observed next to a first generation Apple iPad. The many other artifacts aid in supporting the information presented that explains the rise of science and technology.

Birthing the Personal Computer

Exhibit 2 Photograph*

Learn about the people and technology that helped push the creation of the first personal computer. This exhibit includes information on the first microprocessor, semiconductor, Jonathan A. Titus, Henry Edward Roberts, Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniac, and more.

Electricity and Early Analog Communications

Exhibit 3 Photograph*

This exhibit illustrates the influence electricity and early analog communications had on computing. It contains information on Nikola Tesla, Grace Hopper, early hard drives, phonographs, and much more. This exhibit also includes original Civil War telegrams.

NASA’s Influence on Computing

Exhibit 4 Photograph*

Learn about NASA’s role in accelerating the miniaturization of electronics. This exhibit includes information on the Apollo 11 moon mission, consumer electronics during the time of the Apollo 11 mission, the UNIVAV 418-II, and more. It also contains the last surviving mainframe used by NASA for the Apollo 11 mission.

Perception and Neural Computers

Exhibit 5 Photograph*

This exhibit compares human brains and neurons to neural computers. It delivers information on the human brain, quantum computing, the first neural computer, and more.

Enigma

Exhibit 6 Photograph*

Learn about the role computers played in World War II, specifically relating to the enigma machines. This exhibit includes information on cryptography, the Herbern Code Machine, Alan Turing, the Desch Bombe, the Turing Bombe, and more.

AI and Robotics

Exhibit 6 Photograph

This exhibit displays many different types of robots ranging from a Roomba vacuum cleaner to Robby the Robot. It explains the different types of robots and has a short video on the Turing Test. It also conveys information on AI and the potential effects it will have in the future.
